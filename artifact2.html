The Evolution of Artificial Intelligence: A Visual Timeline

AIML-500 · Machine Learning Fundamentals · Discussion Timeline Activity

Introduction

A researched, designed presentation tracing AI's development from Alan Turing's 1940s codebreaking work to the generative AI boom of the 2020s, built for a Machine Learning Fundamentals course activity.

Description

A 14-slide visual timeline spanning 1939 to 2026, covering AI's key eras: foundational breakthroughs, both AI winters, Deep Blue's chess victory, the deep learning "Big Bang" of 2012, the rise of Transformers, and the ChatGPT-driven generative AI explosion. Every slide carries a to-scale visual timeline strip showing exactly where that era sits in the full history, and the deck closes with a "Key Figures and Companies" reference slide and a full source list.

01 · a1b2c3d 1939-1950
The Foundations: codebreaking to the Turing Test
Turing's Enigma-cracking machine, the invention of the transistor at Bell Labs, Turing's 1950 "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" paper, and Claude Shannon's maze-solving mechanical mouse, Theseus.
02 · e4f5g6h 1951-1966
AI gets a name, and its first chatbot
Minsky's SNARC neural-network machine, the 1956 Dartmouth Conference that coined "artificial intelligence," and Weizenbaum's ELIZA, the first chatbot.
03 · i7j8k9l 1969-1980
The first AI winter
Minsky and Papert's "Perceptrons" and the UK's 1973 Lighthill Report cooled enthusiasm and cut funding for over a decade.
04 · m1n2o3p 1971-1986
Personal computing arrives, backpropagation reboots AI
Intel's 4004 microprocessor, the Apple/Microsoft-driven PC boom, and Hinton's popularization of backpropagation, the algorithm underlying nearly all modern neural networks.
05 · q4r5s6t 1987-1993
The second AI winter
The expert-systems boom proved too costly to scale, and the LISP machine market collapsed against cheaper general-purpose computers.
06 · u7v8w9x 1997
Deep Blue defeats Kasparov
IBM's chess victory previewed the formula behind future AI breakthroughs: large data, strong algorithms, and serious compute.
07 · y1z2a3b 2006-2012
The Big Data era and AI's "Big Bang"
ImageNet's launch and AlexNet's 2012 ImageNet win, powered by Nvidia GPUs, reignited deep learning and reshaped Nvidia's business.
08 · c4d5e6f 2014-2016
GANs, DeepMind, and AlphaGo
Goodfellow's GANs, Google's acquisition of DeepMind, OpenAI's founding, and AlphaGo's defeat of Lee Sedol.
09 · g7h8i9j 2017-2020
Transformers and the road to GPT
Google's Transformer architecture, OpenAI's early GPT models, and Microsoft's multibillion-dollar investment in OpenAI.
10 · k1l2m3n 2022
ChatGPT and the generative AI explosion
ChatGPT reaches 100 million users within months, alongside a wave of generative tools across audio, image, and video.
11 · o4p5q6r 2023-2026
Where we are now, and what comes next
Compute investment and training-compute growth accelerate, with quantum AI flagged as an emerging next frontier.

Objective

To research and synthesize AI's historical development from assigned sources into a clear, chronological narrative, and to practice explaining complex technical history in a form accessible to a non-specialist audience.

Process

  • Reviewed the assigned source material: a YouTube documentary-style video history of AI, an IEEE Computer Society technical overview, and an Our World in Data analysis of AI compute trends.
  • Used ChatGPT to help organize the assigned research, identify major milestones, and draft concise initial descriptions.
  • Reviewed the source video transcript and articles myself and selected the final structure and content, rather than accepting the AI-organized draft as-is.
  • Finalized the design, including a to-scale visual timeline strip appearing on every slide so viewers always see where that era sits in the full 1939 to 2026 history, plus a closing "Key Figures and Companies" reference slide.

Tools and Technologies Used

ChatGPT for research organization and drafting support; Microsoft PowerPoint (via Google Slides export) for final design and delivery.

Value Proposition

This artifact demonstrates my ability to research a technical domain, synthesize multiple sources into a coherent narrative, use generative AI as a drafting aid while retaining ownership of structure and accuracy, and present technical and historical material visually for a general audience, skills directly relevant to communicating data and ML work to non-technical stakeholders.

Unique Value

Rather than treating AI tools as a black box, I used ChatGPT to organize assigned research while personally reviewing the source material and selecting the final structure and content myself, a habit of ownership and verification that matters increasingly as AI-generated content becomes ubiquitous.

Relevance

For Product Manager and AI-adjacent audiences, this artifact shows the research, synthesis, and storytelling skill needed to brief stakeholders on a fast-moving technical space accurately, a different but equally necessary skill from the hands-on build work shown in Artifact 1.
